Key Policy Explorer

Beyond the headline numbers, Reform UK's platform contains a wide range of specific proposals across the economy, society, and governance. Use the tabs below to explore the details of their "common sense" revolution.

The Growth Agenda: High-Carbon, Low-Regulation

The strategy for growth is built on scrapping Net Zero targets, fast-tracking North Sea oil and gas, and aggressive deregulation of business and labour laws. This high-carbon model risks trade friction with key partners like the EU and US who are pursuing green industrial strategies.

Taxation

Key proposals include raising the income tax personal allowance to ¬£20,000, cutting corporation tax to 15%, and abolishing Inheritance Tax for most estates. The IFS finds these cuts would cost "tens of billions" more than the party claims.

Immigration: A Freeze on "Non-Essential" Migration

The central policy is to freeze most immigration to relieve pressure on public services. This would be enforced by higher taxes on employers of foreign workers and intercepting small boats in the Channel. Crucially, the plan requires leaving the European Convention on Human Rights (ECHR), a move with profound legal and constitutional consequences.

Public Services: A Two-Tier Model

In health and education, the party proposes 20% tax relief for private healthcare and independent school fees. This is designed to encourage a shift to the private sector, but risks creating a "brain drain" of staff from the NHS and state schools, potentially worsening the services for those who remain.

Welfare: A Hardline Stance

Benefits would be withdrawn from claimants who refuse a job after two offers or fail to find work within four months. Access to benefits for new migrants would be restricted until they have been employed for five years.

The "Anti-Woke" Agenda

The platform includes replacing the 2010 Equality Act, scrapping all Diversity, Equality, and Inclusion (DE&I) rules, and introducing a "Free Speech Bill" to combat "cancel culture." The BBC licence fee would be abolished.

Constitutional Reform: A Radical Overhaul

Proposals include replacing the House of Lords and, most significantly, replacing impartial career Civil Service leaders with politically appointed professionals who change with the government. This would dismantle a cornerstone of British governance and concentrate power in the executive.

The Verdict: Ambition vs. Feasibility

The platform presented by Reform UK is a coherent and radical populist-nationalist vision for the United Kingdom. It consistently marries a small-state, low-regulation economic model with a socially conservative and deeply sovereignist agenda.

However, a profound and unbridgeable chasm exists between the party's promises and its fiscal reality.

The independent analysis by the Institute for Fiscal Studies reveals that the manifesto's claim to be "fully costed" is not credible, with a funding gap estimated in the tens of billions of pounds per year. The proposals present a vision of radical transformation with seemingly no difficult choices or negative consequences.

Ultimately, the implementation of this platform would represent one of the most significant political, economic, and social ruptures in modern British history.
